# Discovery Call Script

Use this script for the first real commercial conversation after a prospect has seen the repo, a one-pager, or a product walkthrough.

The goal of the call is not to explain every feature.
The goal is to decide the correct next step with discipline:
- stay on `Community`
- move to `Guided Evaluation`
- move to `Paid Pilot`
- move to `Strategic Rollout Advisory`
- open a selective `Enterprise / Sovereign / Gov` quote path

## Call Intent

By the end of the call, you should be able to answer five questions:
- What real workflow matters first?
- Who owns that workflow?
- Why is SA-NOM being considered now?
- What deployment or governance constraints are real?
- What should happen next commercially?

## Recommended Call Shape

- 30 to 45 minutes
- remote by default
- no product over-explaining
- no pricing negotiation before fit is clear
- no promises of unlimited support or broad implementation ownership

## Opening Positioning

Use language like this:

"SA-NOM is a private-first governed AI operating system for real roles, real work, and real oversight. The open-source baseline is already real. This call is to understand whether you should stay on the community path, start with a guided evaluation, or move into a paid pilot in your own environment."

## Suggested Agenda

1. Confirm the organization, team, and reason for the conversation.
2. Identify the first workflow that matters.
3. Clarify deployment posture, provider expectations, and governance pressure.
4. Understand who owns the initiative and how success will be judged.
5. Recommend the next commercial path with clear scope discipline.

## Core Questions

### 1. Organization And Trigger

Ask:
- What team is leading this evaluation?
- Why is this a priority right now?
- Is this a self-managed evaluation, a production-track initiative, or a regulated review path?

What you are listening for:
- real urgency
- real operating pressure
- a named sponsor or decision-maker

### 2. Workflow And Business Need

Ask:
- Which workflow matters first?
- What happens today without SA-NOM?
- Where do approvals, escalation, evidence, or authority break down today?
- What would success change in the next 30 to 90 days?

What you are listening for:
- one real workflow
- one real pain point
- a concrete reason SA-NOM is better than loose chat or generic automation

### 3. Deployment And Provider Posture

Ask:
- Does the first lane need to be private from day one?
- Is the environment self-managed, hybrid, or air-gapped?
- Are there provider preferences or constraints?
- Are integrations, identity, or procurement review already in play?

What you are listening for:
- private-first requirements
- regulated or sovereign constraints
- execution effort beyond a simple self-managed path

### 4. Governance And Risk Posture

Ask:
- Do legal, IT, audit, security, or compliance teams need to review this before go-live?
- Do you need evidence export, audit traceability, or explicit human boundaries?
- Are there public-sector, banking, or sovereign requirements?

What you are listening for:
- governance depth
- review burden
- reasons the commercial path may need hardening or structured rollout support

### 5. Ownership And Success Criteria

Ask:
- Who owns the first workflow?
- Who makes the go or no-go decision?
- What would count as a successful first phase?
- Do you want evaluation, proof in your environment, or continuing rollout direction?

What you are listening for:
- real owner
- real sponsor
- real decision window
- real success criteria

## Decision Guide

### Stay On Community

Choose this when:
- the team can self-manage
- the workflow is still exploratory
- there is no regulated or procurement pressure yet
- special delivery help is not required

### Recommend Guided Evaluation

Choose this when:
- the customer is serious but still deciding the first move
- deployment shape or provider lane is still unclear
- they want founder-level direction before committing to a pilot

### Recommend Paid Pilot

Choose this when:
- there is a real workflow owner and sponsor
- the customer wants proof in their own environment
- there is a clear 30-90 day decision window
- the environment is important enough to justify scoped paid work

### Recommend Strategic Rollout Advisory

Choose this when:
- the pilot is already successful or the environment is beyond first proof
- the customer wants recurring founder-level direction without a daily support dependency
- rollout sign-off, escalation review, or release confidence needs a continuing review layer

### Open Enterprise / Sovereign / Gov Quote Path

Choose this when:
- the environment is sovereign, public-sector, or heavily regulated
- procurement, legal, security, or audit review is material
- partner or customer-side execution ownership is already clear
- the work is large enough that quote-specific packaging is necessary

## Red Flags

Do not ignore these:
- no owner or sponsor
- no first workflow
- expectation of unlimited support
- expectation of broad implementation without clear scope
- interest in "everything" instead of one first move
- regulated expectations with no willingness to scope or staff the work properly

## Pricing Discipline

Keep this rule:
- do not use price to rescue a weak fit
- do not recommend a paid pilot without a real workflow and owner
- do not oversell enterprise scope when guided evaluation is enough
- do not promise more support than the engagement actually includes

Use [PRICING_REQUEST_REPLY_TEMPLATES.md](PRICING_REQUEST_REPLY_TEMPLATES.md) if the prospect pushes for a price response before the call is qualified.

## Suggested Closing Language

Use language like this:

"Based on what you've described, the clean next step is [Community / Guided Evaluation / Paid Pilot / Strategic Rollout Advisory / Enterprise quote]. That path keeps scope disciplined and gives you the fastest reliable route to a decision without pretending this needs more or less support than it really does."
