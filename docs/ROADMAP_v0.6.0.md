# Roadmap - v0.6.0

This roadmap opens the `v0.6.0` phase after the `v0.5.x` patch-and-polish line.

## Theme

From governed operations toward governed global-local alignment.

## Milestone Goal

By the end of `v0.6.0`, SA-NOM should define a credible, structure-first direction for aligning governed AI roles with regional legal, cultural, and organizational context without fragmenting the core runtime into separate country-specific systems.

This phase should establish one coherent direction:

`SA-NOM Global Harmony Protocol`

The goal is not to claim full global coverage in one release.
The goal is to define the architecture, safe claims, operational boundaries, and first controlled implementation path for a single alignment engine that can later support multiple regions through governed constitutional documents and PTAG-compatible principles.

## Core Position

`v0.6.0` should not try to become a finished worldwide localization platform.
It should define one disciplined structure for cultural and regulatory alignment that stays governed, auditable, modular, and compatible with private or air-gapped deployment.

That means the phase should answer questions such as:
- how should regional constitutions or alignment documents be represented in a governed SA-NOM system
- how does a cultural alignment layer relate to PTAG, authority boundaries, Human Override, and auditability
- what claims are safe to make about cultural and regulatory alignment and what claims are not yet justified
- how can SA-NOM support region-aware governance without turning into a separate product for every country
- what is the smallest believable path from concept to first implementation slice in `v0.6.1`

## Primary Structure

### 1. Global Harmony Protocol Architecture

Target areas:
- define a single alignment engine that can load regional constitutions and regulatory principles without changing SA-NOM core structure for every country
- connect global safety, cultural alignment, and governance enforcement into one layered model
- explain how regional constitution inputs should enter the Governed Document Center and become governed evaluation material

Expected outcomes:
- SA-NOM has one coherent structure for future global-local alignment work
- the repository can explain how cultural and regulatory alignment fits the governed runtime model
- the future implementation path remains modular rather than fragmented

### 2. Safe Claims, Boundaries, And Non-Goals

Target areas:
- define what `v0.6.0` does and does not claim
- distinguish auditable alignment support from automatic legal completion or cultural substitution
- prevent the concept from overpromising on coverage, compliance, or human replacement

Expected outcomes:
- the public concept is strong without becoming careless
- organizations can evaluate the idea seriously without being misled
- later phases can build on disciplined positioning instead of hype

### 3. First Controlled Implementation Path

Target areas:
- outline a realistic `v0.6.1` implementation path for the first cultural alignment layer
- define the smallest useful role of dashboard controls, PTAG principles, and regional constitution templates
- keep the first slice narrow enough to finish before broader global expansion work

Expected outcomes:
- the next implementation phase is concrete and bounded
- the concept paper connects directly to executable roadmap work
- the repo preserves a structure-first product story instead of an abstract vision only

## Non-Goals

- no claim of complete worldwide cultural coverage in `v0.6.0`
- no claim that SA-NOM replaces local legal, cultural, or regulatory experts
- no automatic legal compliance guarantee
- no heavy model retraining or RLHF-heavy approach in the first phase
- no country-by-country product fork
- no major PTAG syntax redesign as part of the concept phase

## Candidate Deliverables

- a `v0.6.0` roadmap focused on the Global Harmony Protocol direction
- issue drafts for the first architecture and positioning work
- a concept paper for `SA-NOM Global Harmony Protocol`
- explicit safe-claim and non-goal framing
- a first implementation path for `v0.6.1`

## Exit Criteria

- SA-NOM can explain one coherent Global Harmony Protocol direction without overclaiming readiness
- the repository distinguishes architecture, future implementation, and non-goals clearly
- the next step into `v0.6.1` is concrete enough to start without strategic ambiguity
- `v0.6.0` reads as a disciplined major-phase opening rather than a vague promise of global intelligence
