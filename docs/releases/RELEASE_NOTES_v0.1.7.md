# Release Notes - v0.1.7

## Release Focus

This release turns SA-NOM's compliance positioning into a clearer public baseline by defining how the project should talk about standards alignment, Thailand-facing readiness, privacy mapping, local legal accountability questions, and future compliance-aware AI behavior without overclaiming regulator-ready status.

## Highlights

- Added a formal compliance knowledge baseline for the public repo
- Added a starter crosswalk between SA-NOM governance posture and ISO/IEC 42001 plus NIST AI RMF language
- Added a Thailand-facing regulatory gap map, PDPA AI guideline map, and local representative readiness note
- Added boundary labels to the Thai banking and Thai government compliance starter templates
- Added a compliance skill-layer contract, response templates, and prompt pack for future compliance-aware AI behavior

## Community Baseline In This Release

- source-of-truth compliance baseline for future AI-answering and commercial positioning
- explicit boundary labels such as `Starter support`, `Legal review required`, and `Regulator filing not included`
- safer public language for standards mapping, privacy positioning, and Thailand-facing regulatory readiness
- response and prompt guidance for future compliance-aware AI answers that stay honest about gaps and human review requirements

## Commercial Direction

Commercial offerings remain separate from the AGPL community baseline and may include rollout support, compliance tailoring, legal-review handoff support, evidence design, sector-specific control mapping, workshops, and regulator-response preparation support.

See [COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md](../COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md), [COMPLIANCE_KNOWLEDGE_BASELINE.md](../COMPLIANCE_KNOWLEDGE_BASELINE.md), and [COMPLIANCE_SKILL_LAYER_CONTRACT.md](../COMPLIANCE_SKILL_LAYER_CONTRACT.md) for the current positioning boundary.

## Upgrade Notes

- `v0.1.7` is a compliance-baseline milestone, not a runtime engine milestone
- the new docs make it easier to explain what SA-NOM already supports and what still requires legal, privacy, compliance, audit, or regulator review
- the public repo should still not be presented as automatically compliant or regulator-ready by default
- future compliance-aware AI behavior should be grounded in the new baseline, contract, response templates, and prompt pack rather than improvised claims
