# Pricing Request Reply Templates

Use these templates when someone asks for pricing before the conversation is fully qualified.

The goal is to stay clear, professional, and confident.
Do not sound defensive.
Do not dump the entire pricing table into an early reply unless that is genuinely the right move.

## Rules For Use

- keep the message short
- anchor on fit and next step, not raw price alone
- avoid sounding evasive
- avoid promising unlimited support or a large delivery team
- keep the commercial model consistent: founder-led, scope-defined, partner-supported where required

## Template 1: Early Pricing Request

Use when the prospect asks "What does it cost?" before you know the workflow, environment, or deployment posture.

```text
Thanks for reaching out. SA-NOM has a real open-source baseline, and the paid path depends on what kind of help you actually need around it.

The commercial path usually starts in one of these bands:
- Guided Evaluation: THB 120,000 to 250,000
- Paid Pilot: THB 950,000 to 1,800,000
- Strategic Rollout Advisory: THB 1,200,000 to 3,600,000 per year
- Enterprise / Sovereign / Gov: quote-defined

The right band depends on whether you need a self-managed evaluation, a scoped pilot in your own environment, or recurring founder-level rollout guidance.

If you share the workflow, deployment posture, and target timeline, I can point you to the cleanest next step.
```

## Template 2: Guided Evaluation Recommendation

Use when the prospect is serious but still deciding whether a pilot is appropriate.

```text
From what you've described, the cleanest next step is usually a Guided Evaluation rather than jumping straight into a broader rollout.

That band is typically THB 120,000 to 250,000 and is designed for:
- structured workflow framing
- deployment recommendation
- private-first fit review
- written next-step guidance

It keeps the first phase disciplined and helps decide whether the right follow-up is community self-management or a paid pilot in your own environment.
```

## Template 3: Paid Pilot Recommendation

Use when the customer has a real owner, workflow, and proof window.

```text
Because you already have a real workflow, owner, and target environment, the most practical path is usually a scoped Paid Pilot.

That band is typically THB 950,000 to 1,800,000, and in more complex cases can extend to THB 2,800,000.

The normal shape is:
- one workflow
- one environment
- one provider lane
- one sponsor
- one closeout recommendation

This keeps the first commercial phase focused on proof in your own environment rather than turning the engagement into open-ended implementation work.
```

## Template 4: Strategic Rollout Advisory Recommendation

Use when a customer has already passed an initial proof phase and wants recurring high-level direction.

```text
At this stage, the better fit is usually Strategic Rollout Advisory rather than another large project-shaped engagement.

That band is typically THB 1,200,000 to 3,600,000 per year and is meant for recurring founder-level direction such as:
- rollout review
- PT-OSS and governance review cadence
- escalation review
- release and readiness review

It is intentionally bounded and review-based, not an unlimited support desk.
```

## Template 5: Regulated / Sovereign / Gov Request

Use when the environment is public-sector, sovereign, or heavily regulated.

```text
For sovereign, public-sector, or tightly regulated environments, SA-NOM uses a selective quote-defined path rather than a generic package.

The scope usually depends on:
- deployment posture
- review authorities involved
- regulated documentation needs
- partner or customer-side execution ownership
- whether the work is advisory-first or includes broader delivery assurance

The best next step is a short scoping conversation so we can determine whether this should begin as a guided evaluation, a paid pilot, or a broader quote-shaped program.
```

## Template 6: Too Early / Not Enough Signal Yet

Use when the prospect wants a number but cannot describe the workflow or owner.

```text
I can absolutely share pricing bands, but the first important question is whether there is already a real workflow, owner, and deployment path.

If that is still unclear, the most responsible next step is usually either:
- stay on the community path a bit longer, or
- run a Guided Evaluation first

That keeps scope honest and prevents you from paying for the wrong commercial shape too early.
```
