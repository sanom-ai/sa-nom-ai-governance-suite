# Compliance Knowledge Baseline

This document defines the first compliance-oriented knowledge baseline for SA-NOM.

Its purpose is to help the repo talk about AI governance, compliance posture, and Thailand-specific readiness more clearly without pretending that the public repository is already a regulator-ready compliance engine.

## What This Baseline Is

SA-NOM's compliance knowledge baseline is:
- a documented reference layer for governance and compliance conversations
- a way to organize what the public repo already helps with
- a way to identify where legal review, compliance tailoring, or regulator-facing work still starts
- a future foundation for a compliance-aware AI skill layer

## What This Baseline Is Not

This baseline is not:
- legal advice
- a regulator filing pack
- a certification statement
- a guarantee of compliance with Thai, sector-specific, or cross-border requirements
- a substitute for counsel, privacy review, or formal control validation

## Recommended Reading Model

When readers use SA-NOM's compliance materials, they should interpret them in this order:

1. Governance and workflow baseline
2. Standards crosswalk and gap maps
3. Starter templates and examples
4. Legal review and organization-specific tailoring
5. Regulator-facing completion, if needed

This ordering matters because SA-NOM is designed to strengthen governed AI operations and internal control posture first. Formal legal completion may still require additional work outside the software itself.

## Baseline Labels

Use these labels consistently in `v0.1.7` and beyond:

- `Included baseline`
  - public guidance or structure already present in SA-NOM
- `Starter support`
  - public material that helps organizations begin faster
- `Partial mapping`
  - an area where SA-NOM aligns conceptually but does not yet deliver a complete control or legal package
- `Legal review required`
  - an area that should not be treated as complete without counsel or designated compliance owners
- `Regulator filing not included`
  - an area where the public repo does not provide a finished filing or official response artifact

## Current Positioning Boundary

SA-NOM should be described as:
- a private AI operations platform
- a governed workflow and evidence system
- a starter-kit baseline for internal governance and control design
- a system that can support compliance work

SA-NOM should not be described as:
- automatically compliant
- regulator-approved
- legally complete for a target industry
- a replacement for counsel, DPO, compliance, or supervisory response teams

## Future Skill-Layer Contract

Any future compliance-aware skill layer built on top of SA-NOM should follow these rules:

- answer from approved baseline documents first
- avoid claiming that a template equals completed compliance
- distinguish framework alignment from formal conformity
- distinguish internal governance readiness from regulator-facing readiness
- state when legal review is still required
- avoid representing draft frameworks as settled law

## Commercial Boundary

Commercial work should focus on:
- legal-review handoff support
- organization-specific control tailoring
- sector-specific template refinement
- rollout workshops and evidence design
- regulator-response preparation support

Commercial work should not be framed as:
- unlocking the right to think about compliance
- turning incomplete templates into guaranteed compliance by license alone

## Summary

The public repo should treat compliance content as a disciplined baseline:
- strong enough to help teams start
- honest enough to show the gaps
- structured enough to support a future skill layer
- careful enough not to overclaim legal or regulator readiness
