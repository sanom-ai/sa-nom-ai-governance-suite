# Local Representative Readiness

This note explains why local legal accountability questions matter for Thailand-facing AI deployments and why the public SA-NOM repo should not be read as solving them automatically.

## Why This Matters

Cross-border organizations may use SA-NOM as a governed AI operations layer and assume that strong internal controls also solve local legal accountability questions.

That assumption can be dangerous.

Questions about local legal presence, local representation, accountable contact points, and country-specific legal responsibility are not solved by workflow software alone.

## What SA-NOM Helps With

SA-NOM can help organizations:
- structure governed AI operations
- keep responsibility boundaries visible
- preserve evidence and escalation history
- prepare more disciplined internal review paths
- support regulator-response workflows

These are useful readiness capabilities.

## What SA-NOM Does Not Automatically Solve

SA-NOM does not automatically provide:
- a local legal entity
- a local legal representative
- sector-specific registration or authorization status
- formal supervisory recognition
- final legal interpretation for cross-border deployment structures

Those issues still need organizational and legal handling.

## Recommended Reading Model For Foreign Organizations

Foreign organizations should ask these questions early:

1. Who is the accountable legal entity for the deployment
2. Who is the local contact or representative, if required
3. Which sector-specific rules apply to the use case
4. Whether regulated or high-risk deployment changes the legal structure requirements
5. Which response, evidence, and review workflows should be in place before go-live

SA-NOM mainly helps with item 5 and supports the governance discipline around the others.

## Safe Public Claim

The correct public claim is:

SA-NOM can support Thailand-facing governance readiness and response discipline, but local legal accountability and representative questions still require organization-specific legal review.

## Unsafe Claims To Avoid

Do not say:
- SA-NOM satisfies local representative obligations
- a self-hosted deployment automatically resolves local accountability
- a governed workflow equals legal presence or formal local authorization

## Commercial Boundary

Commercial support can help with:
- rollout-readiness planning
- evidence design
- regulator-response workflow preparation
- legal-review handoff support

Commercial support should not be described as:
- a substitute for local counsel
- a built-in legal-entity or representative service

## Summary

Local representative readiness is a deployment and legal-structure question.

SA-NOM supports the governance and evidence side of that question, but the public repo should remain explicit that legal completion lives outside the software itself.
