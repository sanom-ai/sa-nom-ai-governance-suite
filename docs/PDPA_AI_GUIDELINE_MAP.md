# PDPA AI Guideline Map

This note captures the privacy-facing baseline for SA-NOM in AI development and use contexts.

It should be read as a starter map for privacy-aware governance, not as a completed PDPA legal opinion.

## Why This Document Exists

Organizations using AI in Thailand need to think beyond generic data protection statements.

When AI workflows touch personal data, especially sensitive data or operational decisions affecting individuals, privacy review becomes part of the governed operating model.

## What SA-NOM Already Supports

SA-NOM already helps with several privacy-relevant governance behaviors:
- explicit role and authority boundaries
- evidence-oriented workflow design
- human-gated override and escalation
- clearer separation of reporting, approval, and intervention paths
- deployment models that can stay private or self-managed

These are helpful building blocks for privacy-conscious operations.

## Where Privacy-Specific Work Still Starts

The public repo still does not complete several privacy obligations on its own:
- lawful basis analysis
- purpose limitation review
- data minimization design review
- special handling for sensitive personal data
- data subject rights operating procedures
- cross-border transfer assessment
- retention and deletion validation tied to the organization's data inventory
- model-training and inference data handling sign-off

That means the public repo should be treated as a governed workflow foundation, not a finished privacy compliance pack.

## Practical Reading Model

When SA-NOM is used in privacy-sensitive AI contexts, teams should ask:

1. What data enters this workflow
2. Whether personal or sensitive data is involved
3. Which human owner is responsible for privacy review
4. Which decisions must remain human-gated
5. What evidence should be retained for review

SA-NOM helps with questions 3 through 5 especially well.

## Safe Public Claim

The correct public claim is:

SA-NOM helps organizations apply governed workflow, human oversight, and evidence discipline to AI use cases that may involve personal data, but formal PDPA analysis still requires privacy and legal review.

## Unsafe Claims To Avoid

Do not say:
- SA-NOM itself completes PDPA compliance
- AI workflows are privacy-safe only because they are governed
- evidence retention automatically satisfies all privacy duties
- starter templates replace a DPO or privacy counsel review

## Recommended Next Step

The best next layer after this map is:
- role-pack-specific privacy notes
- template warnings for personal and sensitive data touchpoints
- organization-specific privacy-control tailoring
- future skill responses grounded in privacy-safe baseline wording

## Summary

SA-NOM improves privacy posture indirectly through governance, evidence, and bounded operations.

The repo still needs formal privacy review to move from internal readiness to claimed PDPA compliance.
