# Founder Outreach Short

Use these short outreach messages when the goal is to open a serious conversation without sounding pushy, needy, or over-explanatory.

The tone should stay:
- calm
- high-signal
- founder-led
- private-first
- operationally serious

## Usage Rules

- keep it short
- mention one real operating problem
- do not explain the whole platform in the first message
- do not oversell enterprise delivery capacity
- invite a conversation, not a procurement process

## Template 1: General Founder / Executive Intro

```text
I am building SA-NOM, a private-first governed AI operating system for real roles, real work, and real oversight.

It is designed for organizations that want AI to participate in real operations with clear authority, escalation, and evidence boundaries, instead of loose chat or unsafe automation.

If governed AI in live workflows is relevant to your team, I would be happy to share a short one-pager or walk through one practical use case.
```

## Template 2: Follow-Up After Initial Interest

```text
Thanks again for the interest.

The cleanest way to think about SA-NOM is this: the open-source baseline is already real, and the commercial path is for organizations that want a more structured route into rollout, proof, and operating confidence.

If helpful, I can send the short commercial one-pager and suggest whether your situation is better suited to a guided evaluation or a scoped paid pilot.
```

## Template 3: Pilot-Oriented Outreach

```text
If your team already has one workflow that needs private, governed AI rather than a generic chatbot, SA-NOM may be a good fit for a tightly scoped paid pilot.

The goal is not to "test everything." It is to prove one meaningful workflow in your own environment, with human boundaries and evidence posture intact.

If you want, send me the workflow and deployment posture and I can suggest the cleanest next step.
```

## Template 4: Regulated / Public-Sector Intro

```text
SA-NOM is designed for private-first governed AI operations, especially where authority, escalation, auditability, and review posture matter.

If your environment includes internal review, procurement, sovereign, or regulated deployment requirements, I would be happy to discuss whether the right path is a guided evaluation, a scoped pilot, or a more selective quote-defined program.
```

## Template 5: Partner Outreach

```text
I am looking to work with a small number of strong delivery partners around SA-NOM.

The product is a private-first governed AI operating system, and the commercial model is intentionally founder-led on architecture and governance direction, with partner-supported execution where broader delivery work is required.

If that split sounds aligned with how you like to work, I would be happy to compare notes.
```

## Short Follow-Up Lines

Use these when you want to keep momentum without writing a long message:
- "Happy to send the one-pager if useful."
- "If you already have a first workflow in mind, I can suggest the right starting path."
- "This is usually strongest when there is a real workflow owner and a private-first requirement."
- "I prefer to keep the first conversation narrow and practical rather than pitch every feature."
