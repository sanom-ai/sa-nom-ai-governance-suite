# Thai AI Regulatory Gap Map

This note summarizes the most important Thailand-facing readiness gaps that organizations should consider when using SA-NOM as an internal governance starter kit.

It is intentionally honest: the goal is to help organizations start well without confusing internal governance support with formal legal completion.

## Current Public Position

The public SA-NOM repo is best read as:
- a strong internal-governance starter kit
- a governed workflow and evidence baseline
- a private AI operations platform

It is not yet a complete Thailand-specific compliance pack.

## Key Gap Areas

### 1. Formal Standards Mapping

Thailand-facing regulated conversations increasingly expect clearer alignment to recognized AI governance frameworks.

Current posture:
- SA-NOM aligns with many governance ideas in practice
- the repo still needs more direct mapping to standards language

Recommended reading:
- [ISO 42001 and NIST AI RMF Crosswalk](ISO_42001_NIST_CROSSWALK.md)

### 2. Thailand-Specific Legal And Regulatory Interpretation

AI governance obligations in Thailand are still evolving across policy, sector guidance, and legal interpretation.

Current posture:
- SA-NOM provides internal governance structure
- the repo does not replace local legal interpretation

Why it matters:
- foreign and local teams may face different expectations depending on sector and deployment model
- draft frameworks should not be treated as settled obligations without review

### 3. Local Legal Accountability

Some Thailand-facing AI discussions raise questions about whether high-risk or foreign-provided AI services need clearer local accountability arrangements.

Current posture:
- SA-NOM does not itself satisfy corporate-structure or representative requirements
- this remains a legal-structure and deployment-readiness question

Recommended reading:
- [Local Representative Readiness](LOCAL_REPRESENTATIVE_READINESS.md)

### 4. PDPA And AI-Specific Privacy Governance

Privacy obligations remain central when AI workflows use personal data, sensitive data, or data that materially affects individuals.

Current posture:
- SA-NOM supports governed operations and evidence design
- the repo still needs explicit mapping to newer AI-and-privacy guidance

Recommended reading:
- [PDPA AI Guideline Map](PDPA_AI_GUIDELINE_MAP.md)

### 5. Regulator-Facing Submissions And Response Packs

SA-NOM now supports audit and regulator-response workflow stories, but that does not mean the public repo includes completed official filing packs.

Current posture:
- workflow support is included
- official responses and formal submissions remain human-gated
- regulator-ready legal content is not included by default

## Safe Public Claim

The safest accurate claim is:

SA-NOM helps organizations establish governed AI operations, evidence posture, and compliance-oriented workflow readiness in Thailand, but the public repo should still be paired with legal and compliance review before being treated as complete regulator-facing readiness.

## Unsafe Claims To Avoid

Do not say:
- SA-NOM makes an organization automatically compliant in Thailand
- the public templates are enough to submit to a regulator as-is
- SA-NOM replaces local legal accountability structures
- draft frameworks are already fully covered by the public repo

## Summary

The Thai gap map is not a weakness statement. It is a discipline statement.

SA-NOM is strong where organizations often struggle first:
- governed workflow
- evidence
- bounded AI roles
- escalation and review

The remaining gap areas are mostly in:
- legal interpretation
- standards mapping depth
- privacy-specific review
- regulator-facing completion
