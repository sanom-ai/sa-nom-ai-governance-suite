# Pilot Proposal Template

Use this template after a qualified discovery call when the prospect is not ready for a full rollout yet but needs a structured first phase in their own environment.

This template is meant to turn interest into a scoped paid pilot, not a vague evaluation promise.

## 1. Proposal Summary

- Prospect organization:
- Primary contact:
- Pilot owner:
- Date:
- Prepared by:

### One-Sentence Pilot Goal

State the exact workflow the pilot is meant to prove.

Example:
- Validate a governed legal-review workflow on the private Ollama lane inside the customer's environment, with human-gated escalation and evidence-ready outputs.

## 2. Why A Pilot Is The Right Next Step

Use this section to explain why a pilot is better than either:
- a loose self-managed evaluation
- or a full commercial rollout immediately

Typical reasons:
- the customer has a real workflow and sponsor
- they need proof in their own environment
- they need stakeholder confidence before procurement expands
- private, regulated, or operational constraints need real validation

## 3. Pilot Scope

Define the pilot tightly.

### In Scope

- target workflow:
- role pack or operating role:
- provider lane:
- deployment posture:
- operator group:
- evidence or review outputs:
- reporting cadence:

### Out Of Scope

- broader enterprise rollout
- multi-team adoption beyond the pilot group
- unsupported integrations unless explicitly listed
- full production SLA unless explicitly included
- unlimited support or open-ended custom build work

## 4. Recommended Starting Pattern

For most early pilots, keep the first pattern narrow:
- one workflow
- one runtime environment
- one provider lane
- one primary role pack
- one sponsor
- one success decision window

Default recommendation when private-first is required:
- provider lane: Ollama
- environment: self-managed private deployment

## 5. Pilot Deliverables

Typical pilot deliverables may include:
- scoped kickoff and architecture alignment
- initial role-pack or scenario calibration
- target environment setup guidance
- provider-lane validation
- governed workflow execution walkthrough
- readiness and evidence review
- closeout summary with recommendation: stop / expand / rollout

## 6. Responsibilities

### Customer Responsibilities

- name the sponsor and workflow owner
- provide environment access or deployment constraints
- identify the first workflow and review criteria
- make stakeholders available for review checkpoints
- provide internal go / no-go decision path

### SA-NOM Responsibilities

- guide the pilot structure
- align the chosen role pack or scenario with the target workflow
- support the selected deployment and provider lane
- help validate governed behavior, readiness, and evidence outputs
- deliver a closeout recommendation for next steps

### Partner Responsibilities If Needed

- support implementation-heavy work that falls outside the agreed founder-led advisory scope
- help with environment execution, integration delivery, or enablement when explicitly included

## 7. Success Criteria

Choose measurable criteria before the pilot starts.

Examples:
- the runtime is deployed in the intended environment
- the target provider lane is validated successfully
- the selected role pack or scenario runs end to end
- required escalation points stay human-gated
- evidence outputs are accepted by internal reviewers
- the workflow owner agrees the pilot reflects a real operating need
- leadership can decide whether to move to rollout, extend the pilot, or stop

## 8. Suggested Timeline

Use a simple phased shape.

### Phase 1: Alignment
- confirm workflow, owner, environment, and success criteria

### Phase 2: Setup
- prepare runtime, provider lane, and role-pack or scenario baseline

### Phase 3: Execution
- run the workflow, collect results, and review evidence

### Phase 4: Closeout
- summarize results, blockers, ROI signals, and next-step recommendation

## 9. Commercial Framing

Use this section to keep the pilot tied to the broader commercial path.

Clarify:
- whether the pilot fee is separate from later rollout scope
- whether pilot work can convert into broader rollout planning
- which items would remain quote-specific after the pilot
- which broader rollout items are intentionally deferred until a later phase
- whether later recurring advisory is likely to be the right next step

## 10. Risks And Assumptions

Capture the biggest pilot risks up front.

Examples:
- provider or environment constraints are not finalized yet
- internal stakeholders may still redefine the workflow
- procurement timing may not match technical readiness
- the customer may need integrations that are outside pilot scope
- pilot success still depends on internal sponsor follow-through

## 11. Closeout Outputs

At the end of the pilot, produce:
- outcome summary
- success criteria review
- deployment and provider findings
- evidence and governance findings
- ROI signals or decision inputs
- recommendation: community path / extend pilot / strategic rollout advisory / broader commercial rollout
