# Roadmap - v0.1.6

This roadmap captures the next product step after `v0.1.5`.

## Theme

Expand SA-NOM from early pilot-ready functional lanes into a broader governed operating model that can cover manufacturing, finance, accounting, audit, and IT as one connected enterprise system.

## Milestone Goal

By the end of `v0.1.6`, a serious evaluator should be able to see how SA-NOM scales from role-based pilots into a multi-function enterprise workflow, with clear next-step lanes for manufacturing, new model introduction, finance, accounting, audit, and IT.

## Product Priorities

### 1. End-To-End Manufacturing Workflow Story

Build the first connected domain map for:
- sales planning
- purchasing
- warehouse
- production
- QC / QA
- store FG
- delivery
- new model / NPI

Expected outcomes:
- role-pack priorities are clear by function
- the product story moves from isolated packs into an operating chain
- demos can explain cross-functional escalation, not only single-role behavior
- new model launch and change-readiness become part of the story, not an afterthought

### 2. Finance And Accounting Expansion

Position finance and accounting as governed review lanes that sit inside the operating chain.

Target areas:
- budget and cost-variance review
- spend and forecast pressure review
- close readiness
- inventory valuation and GR/IR exceptions
- revenue-recognition or accrual escalation

Expected outcomes:
- SA-NOM reads like an operating platform for business control, not only operations control
- manufacturing demos connect naturally to budget, cost, and accounting pressure
- pilot stories become easier to scope for finance-led buyers

### 3. Audit Layer Expansion

Position audit as a governed review layer on top of operations and finance.

Target areas:
- internal audit evidence review
- repeated-override analysis
- external-audit response coordination
- evidence-pack preparation for outside reviewers

Expected outcomes:
- audit becomes a reusable scenario family
- evidence-oriented stories are easier to demo and price
- external-review readiness looks intentional, not incidental

### 4. IT As The Enabling Control Layer

Expand the product story so IT is not only infrastructure support but a governed operational lane.

Target areas:
- IT operations review
- change and release readiness
- access governance and privileged changes
- incident or recovery coordination

Expected outcomes:
- SA-NOM reads like an enterprise operating platform, not only a departmental workflow tool
- the runtime and governance story become easier to connect for technical buyers

## Technical Priorities

- add more scenario-aligned example artifacts for multi-function workflows
- continue extending role-pack fixtures and tests as new packs are added
- keep Human Ask, alert, and override boundaries explicit across all new domains
- preserve the private Ollama lane as the default demo story for every new scenario

## Documentation And Demo Priorities

- expand the enterprise function map with finance, accounting, and NPI
- add issue-ready drafts for the next role packs and scenarios across operations, finance, audit, and IT
- connect manufacturing, audit, finance, and IT into the existing demo and pilot narrative
- keep Thai and English sales assets aligned as the domain story expands

## Commercial Priorities

- make the paid-pilot story work for cross-functional pilots, not only single-team pilots
- define how manufacturing, finance, accounting, audit, and IT lanes can be phased into larger engagements
- strengthen the bridge from discovery to scoped enterprise pilot proposals

## Candidate Deliverables

- enterprise function map covering manufacturing, finance, accounting, audit, and IT
- issue drafts for the next wave of role packs and scenarios
- at least 1 additional role pack after HR
- at least 1 additional guided scenario after HR
- clearer enterprise-level demo narrative across multiple departments

## Exit Criteria For v0.1.6

- an evaluator can see a credible enterprise operating chain beyond legal and HR
- the next role-pack and scenario priorities are explicit for manufacturing, finance, accounting, audit, and IT
- demos can explain SA-NOM as a cross-functional governed operating model
- the commercial story can move from team-level pilot to broader enterprise rollout planning
