# Roadmap - v0.1.7

This roadmap captures the next product step after `v0.1.6`.

## Theme

Turn SA-NOM's governance and workflow story into a clearer compliance knowledge baseline that can support safer AI assistance, stronger template positioning, and future skill-layer work without overclaiming regulator-readiness.

## Milestone Goal

By the end of `v0.1.7`, a serious evaluator should be able to see that SA-NOM has a documented baseline for AI governance and Thailand-specific regulatory readiness, while still understanding the boundary between starter-kit guidance and formal legal or regulator-submission work.

## Product Priorities

### 1. Compliance Knowledge Baseline

Define the first baseline layer that future AI assistance should rely on when talking about compliance-adjacent questions.

Target areas:
- baseline disclaimers and usage rules
- source-of-truth structure for compliance notes
- terminology for starter kit, gap map, legal review required, and regulator-filing not included
- future-ready structure for a skill layer that answers from approved baseline content only

Expected outcomes:
- SA-NOM stops sounding like a generic compliance claim engine
- the repo can support compliance-aware AI assistance without pretending to replace counsel
- future skills can anchor themselves to documented baseline content

### 2. ISO/IEC 42001 And NIST AI RMF Alignment

Create a clear crosswalk between SA-NOM's current governance posture and two important AI governance references.

Target areas:
- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 structure
- NIST AI RMF functions and outcomes
- SA-NOM artifacts that already align
- gaps where the repo still needs more explicit treatment

Expected outcomes:
- buyers can see how SA-NOM maps into recognized AI governance language
- templates become easier to explain to risk, audit, and executive stakeholders
- future compliance content has a standards anchor

### 3. Thailand-Specific Regulatory Readiness

Document the most important Thailand-facing regulatory gaps without pretending the repo itself delivers completed legal compliance.

Target areas:
- Thailand AI governance posture and regulatory movement
- local representative or local legal accountability readiness
- PDPA and AI-specific privacy guidance
- Thai regulated-industry positioning boundaries

Expected outcomes:
- Thai teams can read the repo with fewer false assumptions
- foreign organizations are less likely to mistake starter templates for local legal completion
- the public docs become more honest and useful for regulated buyers

### 4. Starter Kit Boundary Clarity

Strengthen the way SA-NOM describes its compliance-related templates and packs.

Target areas:
- internal governance starter-kit positioning
- legal review required markers
- regulator-filing not included markers
- explicit notes on where commercial tailoring begins

Expected outcomes:
- the repo remains ambitious without sounding reckless
- commercial positioning becomes easier because the boundary is explicit
- evaluators understand what can be used immediately and what still needs counsel or tailored rollout work

## Technical Priorities

- keep compliance knowledge docs separate from runtime claims
- make the future skill-layer boundary explicit so answers can be grounded in approved docs
- preserve the public-safe model across compliance examples and starter materials
- avoid wording that implies certified or regulator-approved status where none exists

## Documentation And Demo Priorities

- add a compliance knowledge baseline document
- add ISO/IEC 42001 and NIST AI RMF crosswalk guidance
- add a Thailand regulatory gap map
- add PDPA AI guideline mapping notes
- add local representative readiness notes
- thread the new compliance baseline into README and docs index

## Commercial Priorities

- make it easier to sell compliance tailoring without claiming built-in legal completion
- clarify that commercial work focuses on tailoring, workshops, legal-review handoff, and rollout support
- support buyer conversations where internal governance is the first step and formal compliance work comes next

## Candidate Deliverables

- compliance knowledge baseline
- ISO/IEC 42001 and NIST AI RMF crosswalk
- Thailand AI regulatory gap map
- PDPA AI guideline map
- local representative readiness note
- issue drafts for future implementation and skill-layer work

## Exit Criteria For v0.1.7

- the repo has a clear compliance knowledge baseline that can support future AI skill work
- readers can distinguish starter-kit guidance from regulator-ready deliverables
- ISO/IEC 42001, NIST AI RMF, PDPA, and Thailand-specific readiness gaps are explicitly documented
- commercial positioning around tailoring and rollout is clearer for regulated buyers
